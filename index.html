Tour Bidar Today!

Bidar is a historic city in Karnataka known for its rich heritage, ancient monuments, and beautiful architecture. It was once the capital of the Bahmani Sultanate and is famous for its magnificent Bidar Fort, which was built in the 15th century. The fort has strong walls, grand gates, palaces, and mosques that reflect Persian and Indo-Islamic styles. Inside the fort, the beautiful Rangeen Mahal attracts visitors with its colorful tiles, wood carvings, and detailed artwork, showing the royal lifestyle of the past. Another important tourist attraction in Bidar is Guru Nanak Jhira Sahib, a famous Sikh pilgrimage site known for its holy natural spring and peaceful environment. The historic Mahmud Gawan Madrasa is also a major attraction, built in 1472 as a center of learning and known for its tall minarets and beautiful design. Along with these monuments, Bidar is also known for its tombs, temples, and cultural heritage, making it an important tourist destination for people interested in history, architecture, and religion.

POPULAR DESTINATIONS

Bidar Fort

Bidar Fort is a magnificent 15th-century fortress located in Bidar, Karnataka, built by the Bahmani Sultanate after they shifted their capital from Gulbarga to Bidar. Spread over 67 acres, the fort is known for its massive red laterite walls, seven grand gateways, triple moats, and strong bastions that made it almost impregnable.

Rangeen Mahal

Rangeen Mahal, meaning “Palace of Colors,” is one of the most beautiful structures inside Bidar Fort in Karnataka. Built during the Bahmani Sultanate period in the 16th century, it is famous for its rich decoration, colorful tile work, carved wooden pillars, and intricate mother-of-pearl inlay designs.

Mahmud Gawan Madrasa

Mahmud Gawan Madrasa is a historic Islamic educational institution located in Bidar, Karnataka, built in 1472 by the Bahmani Prime Minister Mahmud Gawan. It was once a renowned center of learning, attracting scholars and students from different parts of India and abroad to study subjects like theology, philosophy, mathematics, and science.

About

Rates

The entry rate for Bidar Fort is very affordable for Indian tourists. Children and students may get concessions. Foreign tourists have a slightly higher entry rate. The ticket allows access to major areas inside the fort. The price is reasonable for such a historic monument.

Fees

An entry fee is collected at the main gate. Separate charges may apply for professional cameras. Parking fees are also available near the fort. Tickets are usually purchased directly at the entrance. The fees are used for maintenance and preservation.

Popular Sites

Bidar Fort is one of the most popular historical destinations in Karnataka. It attracts tourists for its grand architecture and history. Visitors come to see Rangin Mahal and other ancient structures. The fort is famous for photography and sightseeing. It is well connected by road and rail.

Features

The fort has massive walls and impressive gateways. It includes beautiful palaces, mosques, and gardens inside. The architecture reflects Persian and Islamic styles. The fort offers a panoramic view of the surrounding area. It is a perfect place for history lovers and travelers
